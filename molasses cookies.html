The Best Molasses Cookies in the Universe and All Alternate Dimensions

Description

These are the best molasses cookies that will ever exist. They are a part of my soul. I have been eating them since my mother made them for me as a child. I always thought she got the recipe from a special cookbook authored by a professional baker, but it turns out they are actually a Pillsbury recipe. No shade; I'm just surprised.

Also...Pillsbury calls them "Frosted Ginger Cutouts"

These cookies are superior with their heavy molasses flavor and smooth surface. The icing is also a work of art with its sugary shell and chalky but not dry interior. If you eat it at the right time in the drying process, the inside has the consistency of a marshmallow. Who hates marshmallows? Nobody.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup shortening
  • 1 cup molasses
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon cloves
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 1 envelope unflavored gelatin
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 3/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla

Steps

  1. In large bowl, combine shortening and molasses; blend well. Add all remaining cookie ingredients; mix well. Cover with plastic wrap; refrigerate at least 2 hours for easier handling. 2
  2. Heat oven to 350°F. On well-floured surface, roll dough to 1/4-inch thickness. Cut with floured 2 1/2-inch round cookie cutter. Place 1 inch apart on ungreased cookie sheets.
  3. Bake at 350°F. for 6 to 9 minutes or until set. Cool 1 minute; remove from cookie sheets. Cool 10 minutes or until completely cooled.
  4. In 2-quart saucepan, combine water and gelatin; let stand 5 minutes. Stir in sugar; bring to a full rolling boil. Reduce heat to medium; simmer 10 minutes without stirring. Temperature should read 220°F. on candy thermometer. Remove from heat. Add powdered sugar; beat with electric mixer at low speed until foamy. Add baking powder and vanilla; beat on low speed for 5 minutes or until glossy and of spreading consistency. 5
  5. Spread frosting on underside of each cookie to within 1/8 inch of edges. If desired, decorate cookies. Let stand until frosting is set before storing, about 2 hours.
